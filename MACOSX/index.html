About
The map defines three congestion zones in Belfast. Pollution levels are recovered in two categories. PM2.5 is emitted from vehicle fumes and is hazardous to our health. PM10 is caused by general dust particles found in the air and is less hazardous to our health. A vehicle user is charged based on the levels of PM2.5 only. The pollution level is recorded and assigned a colour (red, amber or green) based on an agreed threshold of pollution volume. Charges are aligned to these colours, detailed in the attached table. These zones are being up-dated in real time. Individual sensors readings are available by clicking on the markers. Historical data is available to provide users with a backlog of pollution trends over time.
Information
This software system, is an example of how Belfast can become a smarter city. Air quality is collected and displayed in real time by sensors.
